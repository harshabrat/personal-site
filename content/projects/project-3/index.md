Short notes on making charts easier to read and easier to trust.

<!-- more -->

Good visualizations make comparison feel natural. I try to use chart types that match the question, keep labels visible, and avoid decoration that competes with the data.

For future writeups, this page can become a home for before-and-after chart examples, notes on choosing encodings, and reminders about common mistakes such as truncated axes or unlabeled units.
